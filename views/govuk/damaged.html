1. Definition of a damaged passport

A damaged passport is one that can't be used as proof of identity.

Your passport is damaged if:

  • you can't read any of your details
  • any pages are missing
  • there's significant water damage
  • a spillage covers information on any page
  • the laminate on the photo page is coming away
  • the photo page is discoloured
  • the chip is damaged or showing through the back cover (e-passports only)

Examples of damaged passports






You may not be allowed to leave or enter the UK if you travel with a damaged passport.
