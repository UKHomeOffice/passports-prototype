1. Definition of a damaged passport

A damaged passport is one which isnâ€™t in a condition to be accepted as proof of identity.

Your passport is damaged if:

  • you can't read any of your details, stamps or visas
  • any of the pages are missing or detached
  • there's significant water damage
  • the laminate on your photo is coming away
  • the photo page is no longer the original colour
  • the chip is damaged or showing through the back cover (e-passports only)
